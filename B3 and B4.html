Circle Properties Discovery Interactives

A discovery sequence for Property B3 and Property B4. Students reveal one idea at a time, discuss with a partner, and then connect the observations to the formal property.

Applet 1

Discover Property B3: Angle in a Semicircle

Start with the diameter AB. Reveal ∠AOB first, then ∠AXB. Use the questions to guide discussion before showing point T.

Step 1: Reveal the first two angles

Question 1

Is the circle property “angle at centre is twice the angle at circumference” observed here? Discuss with your partner.

An angle in a semicircle is always a right angle. (rt. ∠ in semicircle)

Question 2

Does the 90° angle work on the opposite side of the semicircle too? Discuss with your partner.

Step 2: Test the opposite side

Take note that 90° works on opposite sides of a semicircle. We will now link this to Property B4. Go on to the second applet to explore Property B4.

Last step

Drag A or B to rotate the diameter. Drag X to test the angle in different positions. T appears on the opposite side of the semicircle.
Applet 2

Discover Property B4: Angles in Opposite Segments

Move the vertices of the cyclic quadrilateral and observe how each opposite angle pair changes while its sum remains 180°.

Question 1

Drag X or Y. What do you notice about ∠XAY and ∠XBY? Discuss with your partner.

Step 1: Reveal the opposite pair affected by X and Y

This pair changes when X or Y is dragged because X and Y form the chord used by the two opposite angles.

Question 2

Drag A or B. What do you notice about ∠AXB and ∠AYB? Discuss with your partner.

Step 2: Reveal the opposite pair affected by A and B

This pair changes when A or B is dragged because A and B form the chord used by the two opposite angles.
Property B4: Angles in opposite segments are supplementary. (∠s in opp. segments)

Last step

All four points are draggable, similar to a GeoGebra exploration. The applet keeps the cyclic order A-X-B-Y so the quadrilateral stays cyclic and convex.
